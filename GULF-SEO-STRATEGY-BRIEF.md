# Gulf SEO Strategy Brief — healingwithattia.com
### Expanding into the UAE and Saudi Arabia
**Prepared:** 21 June 2026 · **Focus markets:** UAE (Dubai/Abu Dhabi) + Saudi Arabia (Riyadh/Jeddah) · **Positioning:** Broad expat + Pakistani diaspora

---

## 1. Executive summary

Your Gulf footprint today is almost nothing — but the few data points you have are the strongest on the whole site. In the last 90 days Saudi Arabia sent 3 clicks on just 10 impressions (a 30% CTR) and the UAE sent 2 clicks on 21 impressions (9.5% CTR) at an average position of 5.9. Both numbers beat your Pakistan CTR (5.9%) and mirror the pattern that already makes your UK diaspora traffic your most valuable segment: small volume, very high intent, people actively searching for *someone like Attia*.

The problem is supply, not demand. You currently have **zero pages built for the Gulf.** Every Gulf impression you're getting is your Pakistan-focused content ranking accidentally. Competitors who *have* built dedicated pages — thepsychwellbeing.com runs separate "Pakistani psychologist in Dubai," "...in Riyadh," and "...in Jeddah" pages — are capturing the searches you're leaving on the table.

The recommendation is to treat the Gulf exactly like you treated the UK: build dedicated, geo-targeted landing pages around a diaspora-first message, widened to include the broader expat market. The single biggest unfair advantage you have is price — therapy in Dubai averages around USD 200/session and the cheapest mainstream option (BetterHelp) runs roughly AED 950–1,320/month, while your sessions are PKR 5,000 (~USD 18). For the large Pakistani working- and middle-class expat population in the Gulf, that is a category-defining value proposition that no UAE- or Saudi-licensed clinic can match.

## 3. Strategic positioning

You asked for a **broad expat + diaspora** angle. Here is how to balance the two without diluting the message.

**Core wedge (diaspora):** "A Pakistani therapist who understands your world — online, in Urdu & English." This is the proven UK message and it should anchor every Gulf page. It converts because it speaks to cultural fluency (family expectations, *rishta* pressure, the guilt and isolation of living abroad, faith) that local Western-trained or Arabic-only clinics cannot credibly claim.

**Broadening layer (expat):** wrap the diaspora core in language that also catches the wider English-speaking expat searching "online therapy Dubai" or "English speaking therapist UAE." Do this with a dedicated section on each page ("For expats across the UAE / Saudi Arabia") and with secondary keywords, *without* changing the H1. You get reach without losing the conversion edge.

**The price lever — use it openly.** Dubai is among the most expensive therapy markets on earth. A clear, honest "Affordable — even from Dubai" section (you already have this exact section on the UK page) is your strongest differentiator. Show the contrast plainly: local clinics charge AED 400–750+ per session; Attia is a flat, low fee with no subscription. For the budget-conscious diaspora worker sending money home, this is decisive.

> **E-E-A-T accuracy guardrail — important.** The highest-volume Gulf term is "Pakistani **psychologist** in Dubai/Riyadh." Attia is an **Integrative Psychotherapist**, not a psychologist, and the site must never claim otherwise (it's both inaccurate and a regulated-title risk). Target "**therapist**," "**psychotherapist**," "**counsellor**," and "**Urdu-speaking therapist**" instead. You will concede some raw volume on the "psychologist" term, but you protect credibility and avoid a trust/legal problem. You can still *answer* the psychologist query honestly in FAQ copy ("Looking for a psychologist? Here's the difference between a psychotherapist and a psychologist, and how to choose…") to capture that intent without misrepresenting the title.

## 7. On-page & schema specifics

**Titles & metas** (keep ≤60 / ≤155 chars, your established standard):
- Dubai title: `Pakistani & Urdu Therapist in Dubai | Online Therapy UAE` — meta leading with cultural fit + affordability + WhatsApp booking.
- Saudi title: `Pakistani Therapist Saudi Arabia | Urdu Online Counselling` — meta naming Riyadh & Jeddah.

**Structured data per page** (you already use these types elsewhere — reuse):
- `MedicalBusiness`/`ProfessionalService` with **`areaServed`** set to the country and key cities (Dubai, Abu Dhabi / Riyadh, Jeddah), `availableLanguage` = Urdu, English, Punjabi, and `priceRange`.
- `Person` schema for Attia with `jobTitle: "Integrative Psychotherapist"` and her credentials/`alumniOf`.
- `FAQPage` schema on the FAQ block.
- `BreadcrumbList` (consistent with the rest of the site).

**Currency / localization cues:** mention session price in **AED and SAR equivalents** alongside PKR in the price section and in `Offer` schema where shown — it reduces friction and reinforces the affordability message. (No `hreflang` needed — these are English-language pages targeting an English/Urdu audience by geo and intent, not language variants of one another.)

**Geo-signals without a physical address:** do **not** invent a Dubai/Riyadh address or fake a Google Business Profile — that's a trust and policy risk. Instead lean on `areaServed`, city names in body copy, local context, and Gulf testimonials to earn "near me" relevance honestly.

## 9. Compliance & risk notes (light, not legal advice)

Online cross-border therapy into the Gulf is common and broadly practiced, but a few guardrails protect the brand:

- **Title accuracy** (covered in §3) — never use "psychologist" or "Dr." for clinical positioning; "Integrative Psychotherapist" is correct and defensible.
- **Don't claim local licensure** you don't hold (e.g. DHA/DHCC in Dubai, or Saudi NHIC). Frame clearly as *online therapy delivered from Pakistan by a UK-CPCAB-trained psychotherapist* — that's honest and still ranks.
- **Data privacy:** Saudi (PDPL) and UAE have data-protection regimes; your existing privacy-policy + confidential, secure (Zoom) delivery already covers the essentials. A one-line note that sessions are private and data is handled per your privacy policy is enough on-page.
- For anything beyond marketing positioning (insurance, indemnity across jurisdictions), suggest Attia get a quick professional-body view — that's a business decision, not an SEO one.
